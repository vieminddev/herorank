FAQ

## Frequently asked questions.

How many tags should I use for my Etsy listing?

Etsy allows up to 13 tags per listing. The tool tracks your selection count as you choose. Use all 13 — every empty slot is a missed opportunity to show up in search.

What do the competition and search volume colors mean?

Green means low competition or high search volume, yellow means medium, and red means high competition or low search volume. Aim for green-on-green when you can.

Should I only use tags marked in green?

Not necessarily. Green tags often perform better, but relevance comes first. If a red tag accurately describes your item, it's better than an irrelevant green one — buyers searching for your product still need to find it.

What's the difference between tags, materials, and styles?

Tags are free-form keywords — your primary SEO lever. Materials describe what the item's made from (sterling silver, cotton, oak). Styles describe the aesthetic (minimalist, boho, modern) and are picked from preset options. All three are structured attributes Etsy uses to match your listing to buyer filters — fill them all to maximize discoverability.

Can I search for tags in different countries?

Yes. Use the location dropdown next to the search bar to switch markets — the search volumes, competition, and pricing all reflect what buyers in that country see.

What does the monthly trend graph show?

12 months of search volume on a single chart so you can spot seasonal patterns and tell whether interest in a keyword is rising, falling, or steady. Use it to plan inventory and time product launches.

How should I use the price range information?

Bargain, midrange, and premium ranges show what other sellers charge for similar items. Use them to position your listing in the right zone — too far above and buyers skip you; too far below and you're leaving money on the table.

What does the warning symbol next to some tags mean?

A warning symbol means a keyword may be trademarked. Using a trademarked term in your listing can lead to violations or removal — research these tags carefully and confirm you have the right to use them before adding them.
