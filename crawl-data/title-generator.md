FAQ

## Frequently asked questions.

Is this tool free to use without an account?

Yes — fully free, no account or credit card required. There's a daily limit on free use; create an account or upgrade for higher daily limits.

What's the difference between title types?

Readable (~45 chars) — concise, easy to scan, great for mobile. Balanced (45–110 chars) — mixes readability with searchability. Keyword-stuffed (110–140 chars) — maximum SEO coverage at the cost of readability. Pick what fits your audience.

How does image analysis help?

We pick up objects, colors, materials, and visual style cues from your photos so the generated titles match what buyers actually see — usually a noticeable accuracy lift over text-only descriptions.

What do the clarity and SEO scores mean?

Clarity measures how easy your title is to read at a glance. SEO measures how well it should perform in Etsy search. Both come with specific feedback on what to fix.

Can I edit the generated titles?

Yes. Drop any generated title into the Title Studio and edit it freely — you'll get live feedback on clarity, SEO, character count, and keyword density as you type.

What separator should I use?

Depends on your brand style. Commas (,) read most naturally. Dots (•) feel modern. Dashes (-) and pipes (\|) maximize visual separation. Spaces help titles match more search queries by avoiding punctuation breaks. Pick the balance that fits.

How many keywords should I include?

Etsy allows up to 140 characters. Lead with your highest-priority keywords and add secondary ones if you have space. The Title Studio warns you if a title is too long or too repetitive.

Should I include my brand name in the title?

Usually no. Use those characters for searchable keywords instead — buyers can see your shop name separately on the listing page. Exceptions: trademarked names or brands that are themselves heavily searched.

What are the synonym suggestions for?

Synonyms surface alternative words and adjectives that might match different searches — e.g. "handmade" vs "handcrafted," "cozy" vs "warm." Use them in titles, tags, or descriptions to broaden your reach without losing relevance.
