Listing Analyzer pulls every listing element — title, tags, description, images, videos, materials — and grades each one against Etsy's best practices. You get a letter grade plus actionable feedback on what to fix first.

FAQ

## Frequently asked questions.

Is this tool free to use without an account?

Yes — fully free, no account or credit card required. There's a daily limit on free use; create an account or upgrade for higher daily limits and unrestricted analysis.

What does the optimization grade mean?

A letter grade from A+ to F summarizing how search-ready and buyer-clear the listing is. It blends signals across SEO, content, and media. Higher grades mean the listing is more discoverable and easier for buyers to understand.

Can I analyze my competitors' listings?

Yes. Any public Etsy listing — yours or a competitor's. Use the same data to see what's working in your niche and apply those insights to your own SEO, content, and media.

How accurate are the sales and revenue estimates?

They're estimates derived from Etsy's public signals — solid for relative comparison and trend-spotting, but not exact financials. Use them to gauge momentum, not to set financial projections.

What should I do if my listing gets a low grade?

Expand each section to see specific feedback. Low grades usually mean missing or weak elements that limit search visibility or buyer clarity. Tackle the lowest-scoring areas first — small fixes across multiple sections usually compound into a big grade lift.

Does a higher grade guarantee more sales?

A higher grade improves discoverability and buyer clarity — which both drive sales. But sales also depend on product quality, pricing, reviews, and demand. Optimization opens the door; the product and presentation close the sale.

What's the difference between completeness and quality?

Completeness = filling all the fields Etsy recommends (e.g., all 13 tag slots). Quality = those fields being well-chosen. The analyzer scores both — which is why a listing can still grade poorly even with all 13 tags filled.

Why does the analysis take a few seconds?

The analyzer pulls fresh data from Etsy and runs multiple checks across title structure, tag quality, description, images, and more. The wait gets you up-to-date insights instead of stale cached data.
