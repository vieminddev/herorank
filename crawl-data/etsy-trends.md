FAQ

## Frequently asked questions.

How often do trends change?

Constantly. Some trends are seasonal and predictable (Halloween costumes spike in October), others move overnight thanks to social media or cultural moments. Check weekly to stay current.

What's the difference between trending and breakthrough searches?

Trending = currently popular, ranked by overall search volume. Breakthrough = rapidly rising — they may not be in the top rankings yet, but they're climbing fast. Breakthroughs are early opportunities before markets crowd up.

Should I create products based on every trend?

No. Match trends against your brand, skills, and capacity. Some are too short-lived to develop new products for; others represent lasting shifts. Prioritize trends you can actually execute well.

How far in advance should I prepare for holidays?

2–3 months for major holidays — longer for production-intensive items. Buyers start holiday shopping 4–8 weeks early, so seasonal listings need to be live and ranked well before the rush.

What does the rank change indicate?

A green up-arrow means the term gained popularity since last month, a red down-arrow means it lost popularity, and the flat icon means stable interest. Use this to identify momentum at a glance.

Can I see trends for my specific niche?

This view shows overall Etsy trends. For niche-specific trend data, use the Keyword Generator to research seed keywords in your niche — you'll get search volume and competition for related terms over time.

How should I use trending keywords in my listings?

Weave relevant trending keywords into titles, tags, and descriptions — but only when they accurately describe your product. Don't force unrelated trends; mismatched keywords kill conversions and hurt your ranking long-term.

Why do trends vary by location?

Different cultures, seasons, and holidays drive different demand. "Summer decor" peaks in June for the US but December for Australia. Switching the location gives you the buying patterns of the audience you actually want to reach.
