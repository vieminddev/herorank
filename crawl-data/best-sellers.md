FAQ

## Frequently asked questions.

How are the top sellers ranked?

Top sellers are ranked on a blend of performance signals — estimated total sales, monthly sales, review count, and overall shop activity. Shops with consistent volume across these metrics rank higher.

Are the sales numbers accurate?

They're estimates, derived from publicly available signals (reviews, favorites, Etsy's API). Treat them as directional — great for relative comparison and trend-spotting, not exact financials.

Can I see shops in my specific niche?

This view shows overall top performers across Etsy. To home in on your niche, look at the products and listings within each top shop, or pair Best Sellers with Niche Finder and Keyword Generator to validate niche-specific opportunities.

Should I copy what top sellers are doing?

No — copying lands you in trademark trouble fast. Instead, study the strategies: how they price, how many products they run, photo style, listing structure. Then build your own version that fits your brand.

How often is the data updated?

The dataset refreshes regularly to reflect current shop performance. Rankings will shift with seasonal trends, new launches, and market dynamics — what's #1 today may be #15 next month.

What can I learn from best-selling products?

Best-selling listings reveal real demand, working price points, photo styles that convert, and the title/description language buyers respond to. Note what they have in common — that's the playbook.

Why do some top sellers have fewer listings?

Quality often beats quantity. Some top shops focus on a tight, well-tuned catalog instead of hundreds of products — easier to operate, easier to optimize, and they let each listing accumulate sales velocity over time.

Can new shops compete with top sellers?

Yes. Established shops have momentum, but new shops win by picking underserved niches, offering something distinctive, nailing customer service, and using data-driven SEO. Every top seller started at zero.
